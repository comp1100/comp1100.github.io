# 1‑on‑1 Chat: Week 11-12 (Covers weeks 9, 10, and 11)

**The Final Stretch!**  
You have made it to the last one-on-one interview! This final chat is a bit different. We are going to look broadly at your entire journey in the course, from Week 1 to now. We will also focus heavily on your actual project, your Minimum Viable Product (MVP), and how you are preparing for your final submission.

**It’s normal to feel…**  
Proud, tired, stressed, or all three. The final stretch often feels intense, even when things are on track.

---

## Structure (how we’ll use the 15 minutes)

- **0–5 min: The big picture.** How have you changed since Week 1?  
- **5–10 min: The MVP.** What you’re building, for whom, and why.
- **10–15 min: Final submission & wrap‑up.** What’s left to finish and who’s doing it?

---

## How to prepare

- Be ready to explain your MVP **simply and clearly**.  
- Know your **personal responsibilities** for the final submission.  
- Think about **one key lesson** you’ve learned about teamwork.

---

## Topics to Think About (Final chat)

1. Defining and Building Your MVP
	- What features did you **leave out**, and why?  
	- How is the build going right now?  
	- Are the features you kept clearly linked to the customer problem you identified earlier?

2. Competitors and Business Model
	- Who is the biggest competitor in your space?  
	- What makes your solution different or better?  
	- Are all required Markdown documents (e.g. Value Proposition Canvas, test cards, learning cards) up to date in GitHub?

3. Course Reflection and Personal Growth
	- How has your approach to teamwork changed over the semester?  
	- If you could restart from Week 1, what is **one thing** you would do differently?

---

## Action plan

Cross the finish line:  
Support your team, complete your allocated coding and documentation tasks, and submit a project you’re proud of.
